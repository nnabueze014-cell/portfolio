Project 2 – Front-Desk to Support Operations with Automated SLA escalations

Project Context:
This project was inspired by my hands-on learning journey in service desk operations, workflow automation, and project management tools. Beyond learning tools individually, the goal was to design a realistic, end-to-end support and operations system that mirrors how real technical service companies operate.

Problem Statement:
In many technical service businesses, customer requests arrive through unstructured channels such as calls or messages, leading to delayed responses, missed follow-ups, and weak accountability. I set out to design a system that formalizes customer requests, enforces SLAs, and ensures seamless handoff between support and operations teams.

System Design Overview:
The solution was structured into three connected layers to reflect real-world service operations.

  1. Frontend (Customer Support Layer):
    A demo customer support portal built using a service desk widget embedded on a web page. Customers submit requests which are instantly acknowledged, categorized, prioritized, and tracked under defined SLAs.
  2. Automation & Integration Layer:
    An event-driven automation layer connects customer support to operations. When a ticket is created, automation workflows process the data in real time, determine the service category, priority, and ownership, and generate corresponding operational tasks without manual intervention.
  3. Operations & Execution Layer:
    Operational work is managed in a project management system where engineers and technicians execute tasks. Tasks inherit SLA timelines, include supervisors as watchers, and update status automatically as work progresses.

SLA Monitoring & Escalation:

  • Customer-facing SLAs track first response and resolution times
  • Operational SLAs govern task execution and delivery
  • Automated escalation triggers reassignment, priority updates, and supervisor alerts when thresholds are breached

Key Outcomes:

  • End-to-end visibility from customer request to task completion
  • Elimination of manual handoffs between support and operations
  • Improved SLA compliance and accountability
  • Clear separation of customer communication and execution responsibilities

Tools & Concepts Applied:
Service Desk & Ticketing Systems, Webhooks & Event Triggers, Workflow Automation, Project & SLA Management, Priority Mapping, Escalation Logic, and Cross-team Visibility.
