Project 2 – SLA-Driven Support Ticket Automation

Project Goal: Prevent SLA breaches by automatically escalating overdue support tickets and enforcing accountability across support teams.

Problem Statement: Support delays were not caused by lack of effort, but by poor visibility into SLA deadlines. Tickets nearing or exceeding SLA limits were often missed, leading to delayed responses and reduced service quality.

How it was achieved:

  1. Configured Freshdesk SLA policies for first response and resolution targets
  2. Built automation rules to detect SLA breaches in real time
  3. Automatically escalated breached tickets by changing priority to Urgent
  4. Reassigned tickets to supervisors and pushed Slack alerts instantly
  5. Synced tickets into ClickUp where tasks tracked SLA status and ownership
  6. In ClickUp, due-date arrival triggered a custom SLA Breach Status and auto-tagged watchers via comments

Key Outcomes:

  • Immediate visibility into SLA risks and breaches
  • Faster escalation and response from supervisors
  • Improved accountability and task ownership
  • Reduced chance of unnoticed SLA violations

Tools Used: Freshdesk, Zapier, ClickUp, Slack
