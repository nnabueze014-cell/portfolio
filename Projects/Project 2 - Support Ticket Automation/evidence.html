Project 2 – Support Ticket & SLA Automation (Evidence)

This page documents the end-to-end flow of a support and operations automation system, from customer request intake to task execution and SLA escalation.

1. Customer Support Portal (Frontend)

A demo customer-facing support portal built to simulate how clients submit service requests for installations, repairs, maintenance, or technical support.

2. Embedded Support Widget

The Freshdesk widget embedded in the portal allows customers to submit tickets, select service type, priority, and preferred contact method.

3. Ticket Created in Service Desk

Once a request is submitted, a ticket is automatically created in Freshdesk, serving as the front desk system for acknowledgment and tracking.

4. First Response to Customer

An initial response is sent to the customer, meeting first-response SLA requirements and confirming that the request is being processed.

5. Slack Notification – New Ticket

A real-time Slack notification is pushed to the support channel to ensure immediate visibility of new incoming tickets.

6. Automation Workflow (Zapier)

Zapier listens to Freshdesk webhooks and automatically creates operational tasks based on ticket properties such as service type and priority.

7. Operational Task in ClickUp (Backend)

Each support ticket becomes a structured task in ClickUp, with assigned pipeline, priority, status, watchers, and SLA-driven due dates for execution.

8. SLA Breach Escalation

When SLA thresholds are breached, automated alerts notify supervisors, escalate priority, and enforce accountability.

9. Service Desk Automation Rules (Optional)

This view shows internal Freshdesk automation rules used to manage SLA monitoring, priority escalation, reassignment, and notifications.
